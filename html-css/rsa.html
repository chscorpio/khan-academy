RSA Encryption Explained for 5th Graders

Let’s imagine RSA encryption is like sending a secret message using locks and keys. Here’s how it works:

Step 1: Pick Two Secret Numbers

You and your friend each choose two special numbers called prime numbers. Prime numbers are numbers that can only be divided by 1 and themselves. For example:

  • 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, and 13.

Let’s pick two small ones to make it easy:

  • p = 3
  • q = 5

Step 2: Multiply Them

Now, multiply these two numbers together. This gives you a big number called n:

n = p × q = 3 × 5 = 15

This number is part of your "lock."

Step 3: Find Another Number Called e

Pick a number e that is smaller than (p - 1) × (q - 1), and make sure it doesn’t share any factors with (p - 1) × (q - 1). Don’t worry too much—just pick one that works. For our example:

(p - 1) × (q - 1) = 2 × 4 = 8

We pick e = 3 because it works.

Step 4: Share the Lock

You now share the numbers n and e with your friend. These are like the "lock" that anyone can use. But keep p and q secret—those are your keys.

Step 5: Use Math to Lock the Message

Turn your message into a number. Every letter has a number using ASCII (like a secret code). For example:

  • The letter "m" is 109.

To lock the message, use this formula:

Locked Message = (Messagee) % n

Example:

  • Locked Message = (1093) % 15
  • 1093 = 1,295,029
  • Divide 1,295,029 by 15. The remainder is 14.

So, the locked message is 14.

Step 6: Use a Secret Key to Unlock It

Your friend has a special "key" (called d) that only they know. They use it to unlock the message with another formula:

Original Message = (Locked Messaged) % n

Using their secret math magic, they get back 109, which is the number for "m."

Why Is It Cool?

Even if someone else sees the locked message and the lock (the numbers n and e), they can’t figure out the secret message without the key d. It’s like a puzzle that only you and your friend can solve!

In Real Life

In the real world, RSA uses HUGE prime numbers to make it super secure. But the steps are the same:

  1. Pick secret numbers.
  2. Share the lock.
  3. Use math to lock and unlock messages.

Congratulations!

You’ve just learned how RSA encryption works in a simple way. It’s like a magic trick with numbers, but it’s super important for keeping things private on the internet!
