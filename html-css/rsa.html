Explaining RSA Encryption to a 5th Grader

Let’s imagine RSA encryption is like sending a secret message using locks and keys. Here’s how it works:

1. Pick Two Secret Numbers (The Lock)

Think of two numbers as your "locks." These numbers are special because they are prime numbers (numbers that can only be divided by 1 and themselves). For example:

  • One number might be 3.
  • Another number might be 5.

We multiply these numbers together to create a bigger number. That big number becomes part of the lock!

2. Share Part of Your Lock (The Public Key)

You share the big number and another small number with your friends. This is called the public key. It’s like leaving a lock open but hiding the key to unlock it.

3. Keep Your Key a Secret

There’s a special math rule that creates a secret key. Only you know this key. It’s like the magic that lets you unlock the lock you shared earlier.

4. How to Send a Secret Message

Your friend wants to send you a secret message, so they:

  1. Turn their message into numbers. For example, the letter "a" might be 1, and "b" might be 2.
  2. They use your public lock (public key) to lock their message. Only your secret key can unlock it.

5. How to Unlock the Message

When you get the message, you use your secret key to unlock it. The numbers turn back into letters, and now you can read the secret message!

A Fun Example

  • You pick 3 and 5 as your secret numbers. The big number is 3 × 5 = 15.
  • You share the number 15 and another small number (like 3) with your friends.
  • Your friend writes the letter "m" (which is 13) and uses the lock to scramble it into a new number, like 4.
  • When you get the number 4, you use your secret key to turn it back into 13 and read "m."

Why Is It Cool?

Even if someone sees the public lock (the big number), they can’t figure out your secret key easily because math is tricky like that. It’s a great way to keep secrets safe!

Congratulations!

You’ve just learned how RSA encryption works in a simple way. It’s like a magic trick with numbers, but it’s super important for keeping things private on the internet!
